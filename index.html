Sultan Mehmad Fateh

Sultan Mehmed II, also known as Mehmed the Conqueror (Mehmed Fatih), was a significant Ottoman ruler who reigned from 1444–1446 and again from 1451–1481. Born in 1432, he is renowned for conquering Constantinople in 1453, ending the Byzantine Empire and marking the rise of the Ottoman Empire as a dominant power. Mehmed transformed Constantinople into Istanbul, the empire's capital, blending cultures and fostering Islamic art and architecture. A visionary leader, he implemented administrative reforms and expanded Ottoman territories in Europe and Asia. His strategic brilliance, tolerance towards diverse communities, and contributions to education and culture cemented his legacy as a great historical figure.
